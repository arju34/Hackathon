Living 🏘️

As you enter college, you must be thinking of where to live when you attend college. Or what would be the best option because finding a living space can be tough whether you want to live on campus, off campus. Living with your parents is also another option if the college you attend is close to home. At home, you wouldn't need to worry about rent or utility bills; and there's less distraction at home than in dorms. You wouldn't need to worry about what to eat or endless grocery runs, some colleges, however, do not have kitchens in their dorms so you'd have to get your meals from the dining hall. However, there are still some negatives with living at home. Here I'll talk about the pros and cons of living on campus, off campus, and with your parents.


Living On Campus 🏫

Pros: If you live on campus, you can engage in college activites, meet new people/friends, and focus on your school work. A study showed that students who live on campus have a higher graduation rate than those who live off campus. Going to classes would be easier living on campus. If something were to break, you can contact the college to get it fixed. If you don't enjoy cooking, you can also just get food which cuts down on the time and energy of preparing food.
Cons: There are distractions and limited privacy, even if you aquire your own room you would still have to share restrooms and other communal places with other people. Like I stated above, some colleges do not have kitchen so you'd have to run to dining hall for meals and the food there can sometimes be repetitive and sometimes be bland, so you might need to order your meals. You would have to take care of yourself completely and do all the chores required to keep your area clean.


Living Off Campus 🏙️

Pro: You'll probably have your space and privacy, no more sharing restrooms (or at least not shared by as many), and more space and freedom. Private apartments are usually quieter and have fewer distractions, and therefore, are better for studying. You can make your own meals as you will have your own kitchen. Having a rental history will make it easier to get a place after you graduate, plus you'll have the "real world" experience while living in an apartment. It might be better to live an apartment as sometimes it is cheaper in rent than dorming.
Cons: It can become expensive as you've got your months rent, utilites, furniture, groceries, transportation, and more to pay for. For Internet and cable TV connection or literally anything were to brake, you're on your own and you would have to fix it (or pay someone else to fix it). You'll have chores as renters usually spend more time grocery-shopping, preparing meals, than those who live on campus. There are more things you will have to take care of as now the maintenance and other responsibilities are on you, the tenant.


Lastly, living with your parents, you won't need to worry about distractions or any bills. Pros: save your money, many college grads are choosing to move home to save money. You won't have as much responsibility; and you won't get homesick or be overwhelmed by the transition as the area and climate are familiar.
